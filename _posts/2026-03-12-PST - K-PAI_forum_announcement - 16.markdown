## Talk 1 &ndash; Private AI in Practice - Deploying Encrypted Similarity Search with Fully Homomorphic Encryption

**Speaker**
&ndash;
<a target="_blank" href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/bahattin-yildiz-0303ba190/">Bahattin Yildiz</a> - Director, Advanced Security at Emerging Technology Lab, LG Electronics

**Korean Title**
&ndash;
실무에서의 프라이빗 AI - 동형암호를 활용한 암호화 유사도 검색 배포

Homomorphic encryption promises to enable computation on encrypted data—but can it actually work in production? This talk bridges the gap between cryptographic theory and real-world deployment.

Bahattin will present a high-level introduction to Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) and discuss how it moves beyond academic research into practical deployment for privacy-preserving AI applications. The session highlights LG's recent work on **encrypted similarity search**, a core building block for applications like facial recognition and personalized services, developed as part of a privacy-preserving edge AI system.

**What You'll Learn**

- **FHE Fundamentals** Understanding how homomorphic encryption enables computation on encrypted data without decryption
- **From Theory to Practice** Engineering challenges and solutions for deploying FHE at scale
- **Encrypted Similarity Search** How to perform vector similarity operations (critical for modern AI) while data remains encrypted
- **Real-World Applications** Facial recognition, personalized recommendations, and edge AI scenarios where privacy is paramount
- **Performance Considerations** Computational costs, optimization strategies, and when FHE makes sense

**Why This Matters to N-PAI**

Privacy-preserving computation is N-PAI's foundational mission. Seeing how one of the world's leading electronics companies is actually deploying homomorphic encryption in production systems provides concrete proof that privacy-preserving AI isn't just theoretical—it's happening now.

For engineers, researchers, and founders working on AI systems that handle sensitive data, this session offers actionable insights into what's actually possible with current FHE technology.

## Talk 2 &ndash; Securing Intelligent Appliances

**Speaker**
&ndash;
<a target="_blank" href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/shinjongho/">Jongho Shin</a> - SW Security Task Leader, Next-Generation Security Research Department, LG Electronics

**Korean Title**
&ndash;
지능형 가전을 위한 통합 AI 보안 아키텍처

As refrigerators, washing machines, and home devices become "intelligent," they introduce entirely new attack surfaces. Traditional cybersecurity isn't enough—AI-native threats require AI-aware security.

This talk introduces a comprehensive security architecture addressing threats unique to AI-powered devices - **model extraction** (stealing proprietary AI models), **prompt injection** (manipulating AI behavior through inputs), **agent manipulation** (corrupting autonomous decision-making), and **physical-layer attacks** (exploiting hardware vulnerabilities).

Jongho will present LG's research on **homomorphic encryption-based inference** (protecting models during operation), **AI-aware Intrusion Detection and Prevention Systems (IDPS)** that understand AI-specific attack patterns, and **agent security** validated through AI red teaming.

**What You'll Learn**

- **AI-Native Threat Landscape** Understanding attacks that only exist because of AI (prompt injection, model extraction, data poisoning)
- **Integrated Security Architecture** How to protect AI systems across hardware, software, and data layers simultaneously
- **Homomorphic Inference in Practice** Running AI models on encrypted inputs for privacy-preserving smart home applications
- **AI-Aware IDPS** Detection systems specifically designed for AI threats vs. traditional network security
- **Red Teaming for AI Agents** How LG tests autonomous systems against adversarial manipulation

**Why This Matters to N-PAI**

Every N-PAI member working on AI deployment faces these questions
&ndash;
How do we protect proprietary models? How do we prevent adversarial attacks? How do we ensure AI agents behave safely?

LG's integrated approach—combining cryptographic protection, behavioral monitoring, and adversarial testing—represents the state of the art in defending AI systems. Whether you're building edge AI, deploying models in production, or designing security for intelligent systems, this framework is essential.
